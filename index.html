The Early Years

In November 1988, Anthony and Maxine Nickens bore a child that would go through some of the darkest years in sports fan history. That child was me. My name is Marcus Nickens, and they call me the Day 1 Chiefs Fan.

The early years of my Chiefs fandom started back in 1995. My father, who I owe all of my fandom to, was listening to the Chiefs game as we road home from church. He was not normally a very passionate person on the outside and so when I saw his excitement during this radio broadcast, it caught my full undivided attention. I perked up when I heard legendary color commentator Mitch Holthus utter that timeless phrase "TOUCHDOWN KAN-SAS-CITY!" for the first time. Marcus Allen, the once bitter rival of the Chiefs, had jumped over a pile of defenders into the endzone. From that moment on, I was completely hooked.
The Dark Years

The next year I followed closely along as the Chiefs won the AFC West Division and the number 1 seed with a 13-3 record. I was so excited to see what they were going to do in the playoffs. To my demoralization, I watched them lose very close and low scoring game to our bitter rivals the Denver Broncos. My heart had been ripped out. The next 24 years would be a lot of the same. Great regular seasons, and then huge let downs in the playoffs. The darkest year being when the Chiefs not only finihsed with the worst record in the NFL but also saw tragedy of one of its players commiting a Murder-Suicide.

The Andy and Mahomes Years

Fast forward to 2019, Andy Reid has been with the Chiefs for 6 years and wonderkind Patrick Mahomes is coming off a historic MVP season where he threw for 5000 yards and 50 TDs. The Chiefs are entering the playoffs as the 2 seed which means that they get a bye week during wild card weekend of the playoffs. My previous experience and dissapointmnet as a Chiefs fan didnt allow for me to get excited when I saw that we would be hosting another playoff game at Arrowhead stadium. My fears of huge letdowns started to be realized in the 1st quarter of the playoff game when my team started off down 24-0. But This new and improved Chiefs team should never be counted out. They came back and ended up winning the game 51-31 and eventually became something that I thought my eyes would never be able to see. SUPERBOWL CHAMPS!!! The sweet victory was made even sweeter by all of the years of dissapointmnet and unrealized potential My team was for the first time in my life time, world Champs, and no one could ever take that away! How bout those CHIIEEEEFFFFSSS!!!!
